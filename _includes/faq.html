Most Common GCSE questions

How does the new 9-1 grading system compare to the old A*-F grades?

The new grades go from 9 (the best) to 1 (the lowest). A grade 4 is like the old C grade, which is a pass.

How does GCSE Maths work, and what are the main parts of the course?

GCSE Maths includes topics like numbers, shapes, data, and algebra. Students must achieve a minimum of grade 4 to pass.

What is the difference of Higher vs Foundation, and which one should my child aim for?

There are two levels of GCSE Maths: Foundation (grades 1-5) and Higher (grades 4-9). The grade 4/5 content is the same for both exams.

What are the most important topics my child needs to learn in GCSE Maths?

The main topics are algebra, shapes and probability. These topics appear most often.

When should I start getting ready for the exams?

It's best to start revising early, but most students begin serious exam preparation in Year 10. Focus more on practice exams as the test date gets closer.

How long will it take to see improvements in my child's maths and grades?

Improvement depends on how much effort your child puts in. Most students start seeing better results after a few months of regular practice.
